Solar Energy

Solar power is harnessed from sunlight using photovoltaic cells or solar panels. It can be used for electricity generation, heating, and lighting.

Wind Energy

Wind power is generated by wind turbines that convert kinetic energy from the wind into electricity. Wind farms are typically located in areas with strong and consistent wind patterns.

Hydropower

Hydropower, also known as hydroelectric power, is generated by capturing the energy of flowing water, such as rivers or waterfalls, and converting it into electricity using turbines.

Biomass Energy

Biomass energy is derived from organic materials such as wood, agricultural crops, and waste biomass. It can be used for heating, electricity generation, and transportation fuels.

Geothermal Energy

Geothermal power is produced by harnessing heat from the Earth's core. This heat can be used directly for heating buildings or converted into electricity using geothermal power plants.

Tidal and Wave Energy

Tidal and wave energy are generated by capturing the energy of ocean tides and waves. Tidal turbines and wave energy converters are used to convert this energy into electricity.
